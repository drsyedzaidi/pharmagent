Handling Missing Data in R

Phase 06: Missing Covariate Data & FREM

Covariates are how a population model explains between-subject variability — but covariates
are themselves data, and they go missing. A subject's weight is recorded at screening but not
at every visit; a pooled late-phase dataset is missing renal function for an entire study;
a survey returns with sex left blank. This phase closes the course by treating the covariate
side of the missing-data problem, from within-subject time-varying gaps (LOCF/NOCB) through
wholly-missing covariates (CC, single/model-based imputation, multiple imputation, full ML/EST)
to the Full Random Effects Model (FREM), which sidesteps the whole exclusion problem by treating
covariates as observations. The capstone stitches Phases 01–06 into one end-to-end, missing-data-aware
covariate analysis with a reusable decision flow and reporting checklist.

Learning Objectives

  • Impute time-varying covariates within a subject using LOCF, NOCB, and linear interpolation, and judge when each biases the analysis
  • Enumerate and select among methods for wholly-missing covariates (CC, SI, model-based SI, MI, ML, full-ML/EST), keyed to the missing-data mechanism and the fraction of missing information
  • Reproduce the Johansson & Karlsson (2013) result: MI, full-ML-on-weight, and EST are unbiased under MCAR/MAR; only EST stays unbiased under MNAR
  • Explain FREM's core idea — covariates as observations, covariate effects read from the OMEGA covariance matrix — and why it is insensitive to covariate correlation and implicitly handles missing covariates
  • Execute a complete missing-data-aware covariate workflow: diagnose mechanism → choose BLQ method → impute time-varying covariates → choose a missing-covariate strategy → fit → interpret → justify

Prerequisites

Phases 01–05 of this course: the MCAR/MAR/MNAR taxonomy and fraction-of-missing-information
(Phase 01), missingness diagnostics in R (Phase 02), deletion and single imputation (Phase 03),
multiple imputation with mice (Phase 04), and popPK event-dataset structure plus BLQ/Beal M1–M7
methods (Phase 05). Working R with dplyr/tidyr; reading familiarity with NONMEM/nlmixr2 control-stream syntax.
