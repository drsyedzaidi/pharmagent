PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 03: Effect Compartment and Hysteresis

Plasma drug concentrations and pharmacodynamic responses often peak at different times — a phenomenon called hysteresis. This phase introduces the mathematical framework to account for that delay: the effect compartment (link model), its governing ODE, and the rate constant Ke0 that controls equilibration speed between plasma and the site of action.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this phase you will be able to:

  1. Distinguish clockwise from counterclockwise hysteresis in a concentration–effect plot and explain the mechanistic cause of each.
  2. Derive the effect-compartment ODE from first principles and define Ke0 in terms of equilibration half-life.
  3. Implement a full PK/PD model with an effect compartment in mrgsolve, simulate it, and estimate Ke0 from hysteresis data.
