PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 05: Disease Progression

Most drugs are not given to healthy people — they are given to patients whose underlying disease is marching forward whether or not treatment is present. This phase introduces the mathematical machinery that separates natural disease trajectory from drug effect, quantifies the spontaneous improvement we call placebo response, and applies these ideas to a canonical clinical dataset: HbA1c dynamics in Type 2 diabetes (Frey 2003). Mastering this material lets you answer the question regulators actually ask — "how much of the observed benefit is the drug, and how much is background noise?"

Learning Objectives

  • Write and simulate linear, logistic, and exponential disease-progression models
  • Distinguish placebo response from natural history and encode both mathematically
  • Contrast symptomatic and disease-modifying drug effects in model structure and parameter identifiability
  • Replicate the Frey 2003 HbA1c model in mrgsolve and interpret its clinical predictions
  • Use baseline disease severity as a covariate on drug effect parameters
