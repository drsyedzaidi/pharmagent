PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 04: Indirect Response Models

Many drugs do not act directly on a measured biomarker; instead, they perturb the production or degradation of a response variable that turns over continuously. This phase develops the mathematical and conceptual foundation for indirect response (IDR) modeling, derives the four canonical model structures (Dayneka 1993, Jusko 1994), and teaches fitting against a real corticosteroid dataset using mrgsolve and nlmixr2.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this phase you will be able to:

  1. Explain the turnover concept and derive the baseline condition $R_0 = K_{in}/K_{out}$.
  2. Classify a PD mechanism as IDR Model I, II, III, or IV and write the correct ODE.
  3. Predict onset/offset asymmetry and time-to-peak as a function of drug and system parameters.
  4. Fit a two-stage PK/IDR model to cortisol suppression data and diagnose misspecification.
