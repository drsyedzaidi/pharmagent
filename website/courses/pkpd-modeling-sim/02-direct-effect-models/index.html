PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 02: Direct Effect Models

Direct effect models assume the observed pharmacodynamic response at any moment
is driven solely by the drug concentration present at that same moment — no lag,
no memory. This phase derives each model family from first principles, builds
intuition for parameter meaning through concrete numerical examples, and closes
with hands-on fitting of all three model types in R using mrgsolve and nlmixr2.

Learning Objectives

  • Derive the linear and log-linear PD models from first principles and state the
    concentration range where each is appropriate
  • Derive the Emax model from receptor occupancy theory (Clark equation) and
    extend it to the Hill/sigmoidal Emax form
  • Interpret EC50, Emax, and the Hill coefficient gamma in physiological and
    clinical terms
  • Recognize saturation behavior and understand why the log-linear model breaks
    down at high and low concentrations
  • Fit linear, Emax, and sigmoidal Emax direct effect models to observed PK/PD
    data using nlmixr2 and evaluate goodness of fit
