PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 06: Tolerance and Rebound

Repeated or prolonged drug exposure often attenuates the pharmacodynamic response — a phenomenon called tolerance — and cessation can trigger rebound effects that overshoot baseline. This phase develops the mechanistic underpinnings of tolerance from first principles (pharmacokinetic autoinduction, receptor down-regulation, functional antagonism, and physiological counter-regulation), derives the precursor pool and counter-regulatory ODE frameworks, and puts both frameworks into working mrgsolve simulations with realistic nitrate-tolerance and opioid-rebound scenarios.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this phase learners will be able to:

  1. Classify tolerance by mechanism and predict the qualitative PD consequence of each class.
  2. Derive and parameterise the precursor pool model (P -> R with Kp, Kdeg) and explain why rebound magnitude depends on depletion depth.
  3. Write mrgsolve models for tolerance and rebound, simulate multi-dose regimens, and visualise the time course of effect attenuation and post-cessation overshoot.
  4. Identify common modelling pitfalls (wrong initial conditions, missing counter-regulatory compartment, ignoring PK autoinduction) and correct them.
