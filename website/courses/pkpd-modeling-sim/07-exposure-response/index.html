PKPD Modeling and Simulation

Phase 07: Exposure–Response Analysis

Exposure–response (E-R) analysis quantifies how drug concentrations or exposure metrics (AUC, Cmax, Ctrough) drive efficacy and safety outcomes, forming the quantitative backbone of dose justification in regulatory submissions. This phase moves from PK characterization to linking drug exposure to clinical endpoints across continuous, binary, ordinal, and time-to-event outcome types. By the end, learners produce regulatory-grade E-R analyses embedded in reproducible R Markdown reports.

Learning Objectives

  • Derive and fit linear and Emax E-R models to continuous endpoints; interpret EC50, Emax, and Hill coefficient
  • Build logistic and proportional-odds E-R models for binary and ordinal outcomes; translate log-odds to probability
  • Formulate parametric (Weibull) and semi-parametric (Cox-PH) time-to-event models with drug exposure as a time-varying covariate
  • Assemble a regulatory E-R analysis report in R Markdown following ICH E4 / FDA E-R guidance structure

Prerequisites

Phases 01–06 (PK modeling, compartmental analysis, population PK, IDR models). Familiarity with R, dplyr, ggplot2. Basic understanding of regression and maximum likelihood.
